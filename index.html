About Me

I grew up in Escondido, California (Northern San Diego) a lover of all things tech, learning from my grandfather who was an electrical engineer. Since then, I enrolled in The Wharton School at Penn, in pursuit of a business education to learn that I am still truly a tinkerer at heart. I concentrated on entrepreneurship as my study, with a focus on design, allowing me to explore my true passion in engineering. My interests have led me to take coursework covering basic design principles, creativity, and modeling in addition to my business coursework and computer science.

I enjoy all things tech and stay up to date on the trends and developments through many blogs including The Verge, Engadget, Popular Science, and TechCrunch. My geekiest focuses as of right now are working on my own drone, filming in 360 degrees, and improving my 3d printer.

My design mentality primarily focuses on the behaviors involved when using products. As a student, I also prioritize cost of manufacture. I enjoy incorporating new technologies or repurposing those that are old. I believe if anything has been done before I can surely DIY it, and I enjoy making things myself for the sake of learning. I hope to be building the future very soon.

Besides tech, I spend my time staying up to date on music. I enjoy all genres within the electronic and hip-hop realm. I regularly attend concerts and am known to spend an hour a day on Soundcloud discovering new music. I DJ both professionally and for my own enjoyment.

I am currently seeking a design role after college on the west coast.
